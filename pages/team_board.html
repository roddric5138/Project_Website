TEAM


Janis Astor del Valle - Executive Director

Janis holds an MFA in Film from Columbia and a BA in Theatre from Marymount Manhattan College. She was awarded a 2011 Artist Fellowship Grant in Playwriting from the CT Commission on Culture and Tourism and is a past recipient of the Zaki Gordon Award for Excellence in Screenwriting. Previous positions include: Artist Instructor, Greater Hartford Academy of the Arts; and Director, Wesleyan University's Green Street Arts Center. A proud Bronx-born Puerto Rican, Janis tours her solo plays to schools and colleges, fostering discourse about diversity and acceptance. She lives in New Haven with her wife. Visit her website: www.janisastor.com


Melissa Mangini - Program Director

A graduate of Manhattanville College, Melissa holds a B.A. in Communication Studies with a concentration in film. A graduate of the AmeriCorps Public Allies program, she was awarded the first Young Alumni Service Award for her work from Manhattanville College in 2011. Prior to joining YRM, Melissa was a Program Associate at Public Allies Connecticut, served as the PACT Leadership Development Liaison to Common Ground High School, a MAXX Facilitator for the Consultation Center and Girls Group Facilitator at Riverside Academy. Having worked with New Haven teens around academic and leadership development since 2009, Melissa has come to feel a strong connection to the city and its vibrant youth.


Daniel Vieira - Program Coordinator

Daniel came to YRM in 2011 as a second-year Public Ally and has remained with the us as our full-time Program Coordinator. Prior to his time at YRM, Dan was the Volunteer Coordinator at The Diaper Bank through Public Allies Connecticut. He also helped to develop the I Am Peace project, working with New Haven youth to promote non-violent conflict resolution, and produced a video on similar on going projects in New Haven. Daniel holds a B.A. in Media Studies from Southern Connecticut State University. At SCSU, he worked on projects that merged social justice and media education.


Nathanael Bassett - Artist Instructor

Nathanael is a graduate student in the Media Studies Program at the New School. He has a BA in Mass Media from Missouri State University. His experience includes social media outreach for Democracy Now! and Captured Times Productions, new media production for Missouri State University, as well as consulting for PrisCo Consulting LLC and multimedia production for Rare Breed Youth Services. His interests include participatory culture, media literacy, the public sphere and virtual community, identity and rhetoric. He is also an an associate editor at Itineration, an online refereed journal for cross-disciplinary studies in rhetoric, media and culture.

Mitch Weisburgh

As a partner at Academic Business Advisors, Mitch applies his knowledge of business, instructional design, and systems to help companies align their products and services with academic needs and education market funding requirements so that they can scale and make a difference to kids and educators. Mitch has overseen development of systems, content, curriculum, and courses across the spectrum of K-12, Postsecondary, corporate training, and job readiness. Mitch co-authored the SIIA guide to the Postsecondary Market and is a national speaker at conferences on education technology in both K12 and higher education. He writes the PilotED blog at http://academicbiz.typepad.com and is on the Board of the Software and Information Industry Association (SIIA) Education Division.
